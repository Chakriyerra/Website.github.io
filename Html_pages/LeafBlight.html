Leafblight

Cause:

Plant blight is caused by fungal or bacterial pathogens that affect various parts of the plant, including leaves, stems, and fruit

Symptoms:

Dark, sunken lesions appear on leaves, stems, and fruit. The disease spreads rapidly and can cause significant damage, often leading to the death of the plant.

Prevention:

  • Remove and destroy infected plant parts.
  • Use disease-resistant plant varieties.
  • Practice crop rotation to reduce recurrence.
